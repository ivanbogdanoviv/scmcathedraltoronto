Great Feast — May 11

Patron Saints Day 2026 — Sts. Cyril & Methody, Equal-to-the-Apostles

May 11, 2026  |  Annual Parish Celebration

May 11 is the most important day in the calendar of our parish — the feast of our patron saints, the Holy Equal-to-the-Apostles Brothers Cyril and Methody, Enlighteners of the Slavic peoples and creators of the Slavic alphabet.

The brothers were born in Thessaloniki — Cyril (Constantine) in 826 and Methodius in 815. Both were brilliant scholars, fluent in Greek, Slavic, and several other languages. In 862, Prince Rastislav of Moravia invited them to bring Christianity to his people in their own tongue. The brothers accepted the mission, and before departing, Constantine created the Glagolitic alphabet — the first writing system designed for the Slavic language — translating the Holy Scriptures and liturgical texts. This monumental act gave the Slavic peoples not only literacy, but a language in which they could meet God.

St. Cyril (Constantine) reposed in Rome on February 14, 869, having taken monastic vows shortly before his death. His brother Methodius continued the mission until his own repose in 885. Their disciples — most notably Sts. Clement and Naum of Ohrid — brought the Slavic alphabet to Bulgaria, where it was further developed into the Cyrillic script that bears St. Cyril's name.

For our Cathedral, this feast is a homecoming. Every year, our community gathers to honour the saints whose gift of the written word made it possible for our ancestors — and for us — to hear the Gospel and offer worship to God in the Bulgarian language.

Service Schedule — May 11, 2026

  • 9:00 AM — Orthros (Matins)
  • 10:30 AM — Solemn Divine Liturgy in honour of Sts. Cyril & Methody
  • Following Liturgy — Festive Community Banquet (details TBA)

All parishioners, families, and friends are warmly invited. This is a day for the entire Bulgarian Orthodox community of Toronto. Contact the church office for details on the banquet.

Great Feast — May 21

Ascension of the Lord — Spasovden (May 21, 2026)

May 21, 2026  |  One of the Twelve Great Feasts

Forty days after Pascha, we celebrate the Ascension of the Lord Jesus Christ into Heaven — one of the Twelve Great Feasts of the Orthodox Church. On this day we remember that having conquered death and shown Himself alive to His disciples for forty days, Christ ascended bodily into Heaven and was seated at the right hand of the Father.

The event is described in the Acts of the Apostles (Acts 1:1–12): the disciples gathered on the Mount of Olives, and as they watched, Christ was lifted up and a cloud received Him out of their sight. Two angels then appeared and declared: "This same Jesus, who was taken up from you into heaven, will come back in the same way you have seen him go into heaven."

The Ascension does not mark the end of Christ's presence among us — He promised to be with us always, "even to the end of the age" (Matthew 28:20). Rather, it is the glorification of our human nature, raised with Christ and seated at the right hand of the Father. It is the sign of hope for our own resurrection and the promise of the coming of the Holy Spirit, whom Christ sent ten days later at Pentecost.

In Bulgarian tradition, Spasovden (from "Spas" — Saviour) is celebrated with great joy. Services are often followed by blessings of fruit and natural water sources. The feast falls on a Thursday this year, forty days after Pascha (April 12 + 40 days = May 21, 2026).

Service Schedule — May 21, 2026

  • 10:30 AM — Divine Liturgy for the Ascension of the Lord
  • Location: 237 Sackville Street, Toronto
National Holiday — May 24

Day of Bulgarian Alphabet, Culture & Slavic Literature (May 24, 2026)

May 24, 2026  |  National Day of Bulgaria

May 24 is one of the most beloved holidays of the Bulgarian people — the Day of the Bulgarian Alphabet, Education, Culture, and Slavic Literature. It celebrates the creation of the Slavic alphabet by Sts. Cyril and Methody and the extraordinary cultural heritage that followed.

The holiday has deep roots. In Bulgaria, May 24 is a day of parades, school celebrations, and cultural events. Children carry portraits of the Holy Brothers, students lay flowers before their statues, and choirs sing in their honour. For Bulgarian communities around the world, including Toronto, it is a day to gather and reaffirm the cultural and spiritual identity that the Holy Brothers made possible.

The Sunday nearest to May 24 — which this year is May 24 itself — is also the 7th Sunday after Pascha, the Sunday of the Holy Fathers of the First Ecumenical Council. After the Divine Liturgy, a special supplication service (molieben) is served in honour of the Holy Brothers Cyril and Methody.

For our Cathedral, May 24 holds particular significance. We are named after Sts. Cyril and Methody, and this day — together with May 11 — stands at the heart of our parish identity. We invite all parishioners and guests to join us for the Divine Liturgy and the special commemorative service.

Service Schedule — May 24, 2026 (Sunday)

  • 10:30 AM — Divine Liturgy — 7th Sunday after Pascha
  • After Liturgy — Special Supplication Service in honour of Sts. Cyril & Methody
Great Feast — June 7

Holy Pentecost 2026 — The Birthday of the Church

June 7, 2026  |  Fifty Days after Pascha

On June 7, 2026 — fifty days after Pascha — we celebrate the Great Feast of Pentecost: the Descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles and the birth of the Church. Pentecost is one of the Twelve Great Feasts and, together with Pascha, one of the most ancient and solemn celebrations of the Christian year.

As described in the Acts of the Apostles (Acts 2:1–11), the disciples were gathered together in one place when suddenly there came from heaven a sound like a mighty rushing wind, which filled the entire house. Tongues of fire appeared and rested on each of them, and they were all filled with the Holy Spirit and began to speak in other tongues. On that day, three thousand souls were baptized — the Church was born.

The Orthodox Church has celebrated Pentecost with special prayers and rites from the earliest centuries. Among the most moving is the Kneeling Vespers service (Коленопреклонна Вечерня) served immediately after the Divine Liturgy. During this service, the faithful kneel for the first time since Pascha — a sign that the Paschal season has concluded — and three lengthy prayers are offered: for the living, for the departed, and for those in bondage.

The feast is also called Trinity Sunday, as the coming of the Holy Spirit completes the revelation of the Holy Trinity — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — to the world. Churches are traditionally decorated with green branches, flowers, and greenery, symbolizing the renewal of life poured out by the Holy Spirit.

Service Schedule — Pentecost, June 7, 2026

  • 10:30 AM — Solemn Divine Liturgy for Holy Pentecost
  • Immediately following Liturgy — Kneeling Vespers with three special prayers
  • The following Monday (June 8) is the Feast of the Holy Spirit — Divine Liturgy at 10:30 AM

All parishioners and visitors are invited to this radiant feast. Come and receive the Holy Spirit!

Great Feast — June 21

Sunday of All Bulgarian Saints — June 21, 2026

June 21, 2026  |  2nd Sunday after Pentecost

The second Sunday after Pentecost is dedicated to all the saints who have shone forth in the Bulgarian lands — martyrs, hierarchs, monastics, righteous men and women — from the time of the Apostles to the present day. This feast is unique to the Bulgarian Orthodox Church and expresses our deep gratitude to God for the great cloud of witnesses He has given to our people.

Among the saints commemorated on this day are:

  • St. Tsar Boris-Michael — the Baptizer of the Bulgarian people (864 AD), Equal-to-the-Apostles
  • Sts. Cyril and Methody — our patron saints and Enlighteners of all Slavs
  • Sts. Clement and Naum of Ohrid — disciples of the Holy Brothers who brought Christianity and literacy to Bulgaria
  • Venerable John of Rila — Bulgaria's greatest ascetic and patron saint
  • Venerable Paraskeva (Petka) of Tarnovo — Bulgaria's most beloved female saint
  • Venerable Paisius of Hilendar — the monk who ignited the Bulgarian National Revival
  • The Holy Martyrs of Bulgaria — countless men and women who gave their lives for the faith under Ottoman rule

This feast invites us to look upon our own heritage with gratitude and to seek the intercessions of the Bulgarian saints who have gone before us. We are not alone — we are surrounded by a great cloud of witnesses who pray for us and for all the Bulgarian people scattered across the earth.

Service Schedule — June 14, 2026

  • 10:30 AM — Solemn Divine Liturgy for All Bulgarian Saints
  • All parishioners and members of the Bulgarian community are warmly invited
June 8–28, 2026

The Apostles' Fast (Petrovi Posti) — June 8 to 28, 2026

June 8 – June 28, 2026  |  Ends with the Feast of Sts. Peter & Paul

Following the Sunday of All Saints, the Orthodox Church enters the Apostles' Fast (also called Petrovi Posti or the Apostolic Fast). This fast begins on June 8 and lasts until the feast of the Holy Apostles Peter and Paul on June 29. In 2026, it is a relatively short fast of twenty days.

The Apostles' Fast has ancient roots, commemorating the period the Holy Apostles spent in prayer and fasting before going out to preach the Gospel to all the world. Just as Christ fasted forty days before His public ministry, the Apostles prepared themselves through fasting and prayer before undertaking their mission. The fast invites us to join in their spiritual discipline and to intercede for the spread of the Faith.

During the Apostles' Fast, fasting is observed from meat and dairy. Fish, oil, and wine are generally permitted on weekdays except Wednesdays and Fridays. For specific guidelines, please consult with the parish priest.

Key Dates During the Apostles' Fast

  • June 7 (Sunday) — All Saints Sunday / Apostles' Fast Eve (Petrovi Zagovezni)
  • June 8 (Monday) — Beginning of the Apostles' Fast
  • June 14 (Sunday) — Sunday of All Bulgarian Saints
  • June 24 (Wednesday) — * Nativity of St. John the Baptist (Enyovden) — Divine Liturgy 10:30 AM
  • June 29 (Monday) — + Sts. Peter and Paul (Petrovden) — END OF FAST — Divine Liturgy 10:30 AM

The feast of the Holy First-Supreme Apostles Peter and Paul (Petrovden) on June 29 concludes the fast with a joyful celebration. All who bear the names Petar, Pavel, Pavlina, and related names celebrate their name-day on this day.

Great Feast — April 12–19

Holy Week & Pascha 2026 — "The Feast of Feasts"

April 12–19, 2026  |  Palm Sunday through Pascha

The most solemn and most joyful week of the Orthodox Church year begins on Palm Sunday, April 12, 2026, and culminates in the Resurrection of Christ — Pascha — on April 19, 2026.

Holy Week (Страстна Седмица) is a week of intense liturgical services that walk the faithful through the final days of Christ's earthly life: His triumphal entry into Jerusalem, the Mystical Supper, the betrayal, the Passion, the Crucifixion, the burial, and finally the glorious Resurrection. Each service is unique, deeply moving, and rich in theology and hymnography.

March 2 – April 18, 2026

Great Lent 2026 — A Season of Prayer, Fasting & Repentance

March 2 – April 18, 2026  |  40-Day Lenten Fast

Great Lent — the longest and most solemn fast of the Orthodox Church year — begins on Clean Monday, March 2, 2026, and continues for forty days until the eve of Palm Sunday. Together with Holy Week, this is a fifty-day period of intensified prayer, fasting, and repentance in preparation for the Feast of Feasts — Holy Pascha.

During Great Lent, the Orthodox Church offers a rich cycle of special services not served at any other time of year: the Presanctified Gifts Liturgy (on Wednesdays and Fridays), the Great Canon of St. Andrew of Crete (in the first and fifth weeks), the Akathist Hymn to the Theotokos (on the fifth Saturday), and the Lenten Sunday Liturgies of St. Basil the Great.

Great Feast — March 25

Annunciation of the Most Holy Theotokos (March 25, 2026)

March 25, 2026  |  One of the Twelve Great Feasts

The Annunciation (Blagoveshteniye — Благовещение) celebrates the moment the Archangel Gabriel appeared to the Virgin Mary and announced that she would conceive and bear the Son of God: "The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you; therefore the child to be born will be called holy — the Son of God." (Luke 1:35). Mary's response — "Let it be to me according to your word" — is the great act of faith that opened the way for the Incarnation.

The feast falls this year in the midst of the 5th week of Great Lent. Despite the Lenten fast, fish is permitted on this day — a sign of the great joy of the feast. The Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom is served (rather than the Presanctified Gifts Liturgy).

Service Schedule — March 25, 2026

  • 10:30 AM — Divine Liturgy for the Annunciation (Fish permitted this day)
Great Feast — February 2

Presentation of the Lord in the Temple — Candlemas (February 2, 2026)

February 2, 2026  |  One of the Twelve Great Feasts

Forty days after the Nativity of Christ, the Holy Family brought the infant Jesus to the Jerusalem Temple, fulfilling the Mosaic Law (Leviticus 12). There, the elderly righteous Simeon — who had been promised by the Holy Spirit that he would not see death before seeing the Messiah — took the child in his arms and uttered the famous words: "Lord, now lettest thou thy servant depart in peace, according to thy word, for mine eyes have seen thy salvation." This canticle, the Nunc Dimittis, is sung at Vespers every day in the Orthodox Church.

This feast marks the "meeting" (sretenie) of the Old and New Testaments — the aged prophet representing all of Israel's longing meets the infant who is the fulfillment of that longing. Candles are blessed at the Divine Liturgy on this day, hence the Western name "Candlemas."

Service Schedule — February 2, 2026

  • 10:30 AM — Divine Liturgy for the Presentation of the Lord | Blessing of Candles
Great Feast — January 6

Holy Theophany — Epiphany — The Great Blessing of Waters (January 6, 2026)

January 6, 2026  |  One of the Twelve Great Feasts — Yordanovden

On January 6, the Orthodox Church celebrates Holy Theophany (Богоявление) — the Baptism of the Lord Jesus Christ in the Jordan River by St. John the Baptist. This is one of the oldest and most solemn of the Twelve Great Feasts. At His Baptism, the Holy Trinity was manifested to the world: the Father spoke from heaven ("This is my beloved Son"), the Son stood in the Jordan, and the Holy Spirit descended in the form of a dove. Hence the name "Theophany" — the manifestation of God.

The most distinctive liturgical element of this feast is the Great Blessing of Waters — a solemn rite in which water is sanctified through prayer and the invocation of the Holy Trinity. The blessed water (Светена вода) is kept throughout the year and used for blessing homes, vehicles, and people during times of illness or difficulty.

In Bulgarian tradition, Yordanovden (January 6) and Ivanovden (January 7) are among the most celebrated winter feasts. On Yordanovden, all who bear the name Yordan are celebrated; on Ivanovden (feast of St. John the Baptist), all named Ivan.

Service Schedule — January 6, 2026

  • 10:30 AM — Solemn Divine Liturgy for Holy Theophany | Great Blessing of Waters
Parish Life

Cathedral Choir — New Members Welcome for 2026

2026 — Open Auditions  |  Sundays 9:00 AM

The Cathedral Choir of Sts. Cyril and Methody is at the heart of our parish worship. For over a century, the choir has lifted the Divine Liturgy with sacred song, chanting the ancient Byzantine and Bulgarian Orthodox musical traditions that connect us to our ancestors in the faith.

We are actively seeking new members — both experienced singers and those willing to learn. Whether you have a background in sacred music or simply a love for Orthodox hymnody and a desire to serve the Lord through song, we invite you to join us.

  • Rehearsals: Every Sunday at 9:00 AM, before the Divine Liturgy
  • Voice parts needed: Soprano, Alto, Tenor, Bass
  • Repertoire: Bulgarian and Byzantine Orthodox sacred music
  • No prior choral experience required — a willing heart and a love for Orthodox worship are enough to start

To express interest or to arrange an audition, please contact the church office at 416-368-2828 or by email at scmcathedraltoronto@hotmail.com.
